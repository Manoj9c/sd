Dental Implants, Bowie MD

Dental implants are replacement tooth roots. Superior Cosmetic and Family Dentistry Implants provide a strong foundation for fixed (permanent) or removable replacement teeth that are made to match your natural teeth.


A dental implant (also known as an Endosseous Implant or Fixture) is a surgical component that interfaces with the bone of the jaw or skull to support a dental prosthesis such as a crown, bridge, denture, facial prosthesis or to act as an orthodontic anchor.





Bowie, MD Dental Implants (Consultation, Implant Placement, and Dental Recovery)

A dental implant is an artificial tooth that replaces a tooth that has been taken out. Dental Implants are natural-looking, which can provide support for dentures, and it does not affect the teeth bordering them. Implant are as stable as your real teeth and protect you from the loss of jawbone, which may occur when you lose a teeth.


To determine if dental implants are right for you, a consultation with your Bowie Dentist, Oral Surgeon, and/or Periodontist or Prosthodontist Maryland is required. During this appointment, your Bowie, MD dental professional will thoroughly examine your teeth and gums and evaluate bone density and quantity. Which may involve X-rays and computer tomography scans (CT scans) to ensure there is sufficient bone structure for placing the implant(s), and it is needed to determine exactly where the implant should be placed.


How are Dental Implants Placed?

Usually, our office procedure to place a dental implant takes about an hour for one implant and no more than two or three hours for multiple implants.

The dental placement process consists of the following steps:

  • If indicated, you will be given medication such as antibiotics prior to the dental surgery. You will be offered sedation with nitrous oxide ("laughing gas") or intravenous medications. Then, a local anesthetic are administered to numb the areas where the implant's will be placed.
  • After you are comfortable, a little incision is made into the gum tissue, revealing the bone into which the dental implant will be placed.
  • Using special dental instruments, a socket is created carefully, avoiding damage to the bone.
  • The titanium implant is then fitted into the socket.
  • Finally, if required, sutures will be used.
